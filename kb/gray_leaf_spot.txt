Gray Leaf Spot (corn)

What it is:
- A fungal disease that makes gray/brown rectangular lesions.
- It often grows between leaf veins, making blocky shapes.

Main symptoms:
- Rectangular spots (like long boxes) between leaf veins.
- Spots can be gray, tan, or brown.
- Lesions may join together and kill larger parts of the leaf.

How to confirm:
1) Spots are “rectangular” and often aligned with leaf veins.
2) Many spots appear on lower leaves first, then move upward.
3) Leaves may look dry/burnt when severe.

What can cause it / risk factors:
- Warm, humid weather and long periods of leaf wetness.
- Corn debris left on soil from previous season.
- Dense fields with poor airflow.

Treatment (safe + practical):
- Remove heavily infected leaves if possible (small garden scale).
- Reduce moisture on leaves: improve spacing/airflow.
- If many plants are affected:
  - A corn-registered fungicide may help, especially if applied early.
  - Ask local agriculture extension for recommended fungicide and timing.

Prevention:
- Rotate crops (avoid corn after corn).
- Bury or remove infected crop residue if possible.
- Use disease-tolerant varieties.
- Improve airflow: correct spacing, weed control.
- Avoid watering leaves late in the day.

When to ask an expert:
- If the top leaves are affected early (these leaves affect yield most).
- If lesions spread quickly after humid weather.
