Healthy corn leaf (no disease detected)

What this means:
- Your corn leaf looks healthy. No clear signs of fungal/bacterial leaf disease.

How to confirm (simple checks):
1) Leaf is mostly green.
2) No orange/brown “rust” powder spots.
3) No long cigar-shaped dead areas (blight).
4) No gray/brown rectangular spots between veins (gray leaf spot).

What to do now (maintenance):
- Keep regular watering (avoid flooding).
- Use balanced fertilizer based on soil test if possible.
- Remove weeds to reduce pests and disease spread.
- Check leaves weekly for early symptoms.

Prevention tips:
- Use disease-resistant corn varieties if available.
- Rotate crops (avoid planting corn in the same field every season).
- Keep good spacing for airflow.
- Avoid watering leaves at night (fungus likes wet leaves).
- Clean tools and avoid moving infected plant material to new areas.

If you still see insect damage:
- Look for holes, chewing marks, insects under leaves.
- Insect problems are different from disease. Consult local ag support if needed.
