Corn Leaf Blight (Blight)

What it is:
- A leaf disease that causes larger “blighted” areas (dead leaf tissue).
- Often looks like long lesions, not tiny spots.

Main symptoms:
- Long, oval, or cigar-shaped lesions.
- Lesions can be tan/gray in the center with darker edges.
- Leaves may turn yellow, then brown and dry.
- Severe cases: large sections of leaf die.

How to confirm:
1) Lesions are longer than most spots (not small dots).
2) Lesions may follow the length of the leaf.
3) Several lesions can merge into big dead areas.

What can cause it / risk factors:
- Warm, humid conditions.
- Old infected corn residue in the field.
- Susceptible corn variety.
- Poor airflow / dense field.

Treatment (safe + practical):
- Remove heavily infected leaves for small farms/gardens (avoid spreading).
- Improve airflow and reduce leaf wetness.
- If disease is spreading and many plants are affected:
  - Consider corn-labeled fungicide applied early (before upper leaves are damaged).
  - Consult local agriculture extension for best product and timing.

Prevention:
- Rotate crops and manage corn residue.
- Use resistant/tolerant varieties.
- Proper spacing and weed control for airflow.
- Monitor early in the season; early action prevents big damage.
- Avoid overhead watering late in the day.

When to ask an expert:
- If upper leaves show lesions early.
- If more than 20–30% of plants are affected and spreading fast.
