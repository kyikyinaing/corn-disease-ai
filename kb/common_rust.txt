Common Rust (corn)

What it is:
- A fungal disease that creates small rust-like spots (pustules) on the leaf.

Main symptoms:
- Small round to oval spots that look orange/brown.
- Spots may feel slightly raised and can rub off like powder.
- Mostly on upper leaf surface but can be on both sides.

How to confirm:
1) Look for orange/brown powdery spots.
2) Rub gently: if orange powder comes off, it is likely rust.
3) Many small spots across the leaf (not one big dead area).

What can cause it / risk factors:
- Cool to mild temperatures, moisture on leaves, dew/fog.
- Dense planting (poor airflow).
- Susceptible corn variety.

Treatment (safe + practical):
- Remove heavily infected leaves if infection is strong (do not spread to healthy plants).
- Improve airflow: remove weeds, avoid overcrowding.
- If the disease is spreading quickly and many plants are affected:
  - Consider a fungicide that is recommended for corn rust (follow local label rules).
  - Best is to ask local agriculture extension for the correct product.

Prevention:
- Use rust-resistant varieties.
- Rotate crops and remove old corn debris after harvest.
- Avoid overhead irrigation at night.
- Monitor early: treat early if it starts spreading.

When to ask an expert:
- If more than 30–40% of leaves show rust, or yield is at risk.
- If you are unsure whether it is rust or another disease.
